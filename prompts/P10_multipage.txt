<task>
Given the existing GameSpec and a new scan of the child's changed paper (the
same page edited, or an added page), merge them into one continuous level,
preserving player progress where the geometry allows.
Return only the merged GameSpec (the schema has no summary field).
</task>
<constraints>
- Entities still present on the paper keep their existing ids unchanged.
- New drawings become new entities with new ids, appended after existing ones.
- Marks the child erased are removed from the merged spec: an erased wall
  opens a gap. Never keep an entity the paper no longer shows.
- Resolve the seam (how page B connects to page A) as a traversable link.
- Do not restyle or move existing entities beyond the seam join.
</constraints>
<maze_walls>
When the merged game is a maze, the wall set carries no player progress:
discard every previous wall entity and emit walls ONLY from what the new
scan shows. The merged spec must not contain both an old and a re-traced
copy of the same wall — duplicated or stale walls cover corridors and
falsely seal routes. Emit each straight wall run as its own thin platform
entity (narrow dimension at most 0.06 of the page; a curved or L-shaped
wall becomes several short thin boxes), never covering open corridor
space. A wall segment the child erased must not appear at all — an erased
wall is exactly how a child opens a route, and the route must open where
the paper now shows a gap. Non-wall entities (hero, goal, collectibles,
keys, doors) keep their existing ids when still on the paper.
</maze_walls>
